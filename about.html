Hi I'm Jessica

Welcome to my website! I'm Jessica, a 3rd year at Brown University pursuing a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science. Currently, I'm a full time student as well as a Linux Lab Consultant and Undergraduate Teaching Assistant for Programming Languages.

Recently I've done a lot of work with web dev and frontend UI design(including creating this website from scratch!). I also have experience in iOS programming and functional programming. I really like designing software that helps connect people to people. Feel free to check out my design blog where I write my thoughts about different apps and websites. Other than frontend development, I also have a strong interest in Programming Languages. I'd love to get the opportunity to explore that path in the future too!

For the last two summers I've worked at Facebook. In 2013 I was part of Facebook's first class of Facebook University interns. At FBU I learned iOS development and worked with a FTE mentor and a group of 3 other interns to create an an iOS application. This last summer, I joined the Timeline Team to work on web development. I worked on a new feature for Timeline that has not been shipped publicly yet, and also on a redesign of the about unit side unit on Timeline.

One of my passions is mentoring and teaching others. Other than being a teaching assistant, I'm also a WiCS mentor, and a Organizer of Hack@Brown, Brown's annual Hackathon. I love mentoring underclassmen and sharing the things that I've learned over these last few years. Helping organize Hack@Brown is one of the ways I can do this at a larger scale. Hack@Brown's mission is to redesign the hackathon experience for beginners to be a place for people to come learn skills they wouldn't normally learn in university.
